History of Christian Campus Ministry in Rolla

Christian Campus Ministry has deep roots in Rolla. In 1971, First Christian Church (Greentree Christian Church) acquired a house across from campus at 1208 Pine to use in starting to reach out to university students. A formal board was organized over the next several months from area supporting churches to begin campus ministry and in 1972, Dr. Roger Thomas was called to serve as the first campus minister of dwhat would come to be known as Christian Campus Fellowship here in Rolla.
Residential Housing

One outreach of the ministry of CCF has been to provide student housing. The University granted CCF "approved housing status" which allows students with under 50 credit hours to reside in our facilities. The Christian Campus House is the only off-campus, non-Greek (fraternity or sorority) housing option for underclassmen.
The ministry at one point leased the Carnahan Building at 1310 N Pine (now Sigma Chi fraternity) for additional housing. Our two residential sites were referred to as Campus House-North and Campus House-South.

In 1977 the board made the move to purchase property at 607 State Street, near the post office docks and behind Denny Ford Dealership. This former church building served CCM for the next 23 years housing 19 male students.

In 1999 the property at 607 State St was purchased from CCF by Denny Ford. This enabled CCF to purchase a large lot just 1 block east of campus at the corner of 12th and Oak to begin construction of a new facility adjoining the existing 5 apartments.

In 2000 the new Christian Campus House was completed at 112 E 12th St. The facility has offices and room for 28 residents in the main facility and 10 more in the pre-existing apartments.

In 2006 a computer lab on the second floor residents area was converted to a 3 person room bringing our total capacity to 41 residents.

In 2011 property was leased a couple of blocks from our men's facility at 1400 N. Pine for our first ever Women's Christian Campus House allowing the ministry to house up to 20 female residents.
