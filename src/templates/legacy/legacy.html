Forging a Legacy

A legacy is defined as anything handed down from the past from an ancestor or predecessor. As humans, we all leave a legacy behind. As believers, God tells us in His word how important it is to leave a godly legacy behind and stresses that we live in a way pleasing to Him. The current students are building a legacy with their classmates and professors. Our graduates are building legacies in their careers, families, churches and communities. Students and supporters have done this in CCF over the past 40 years, and we eagerly look forward to generations to come. We see this sentiment echoed in Psalms 61:5:

"You have given me the heritage of those who fear your name."

Mission

Christian Campus Fellowship exists to reach non-believing students, nurture fellowship among believers, deepen knowledge of and love for our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, and to encourage one another to serve and worship God.

We strive to create a godly community for the students. Through this community we endeavor to help disciple, mentor and foster an environment for healthy and eternally beneficial friendships.

History

In 1971, First Christian Church acquired a property at 1208 Pine to reach out to university students. A formal board was organized over the next several months from area supporting churches to begin a campus ministry. One outreach of the ministry of CCF has been to provide student housing. Having obtained "approved housing status", the Christian Campus House is the only off-campus, non-Greek housing option for underclassmen.

In 1977 the board purchased a different property at 607 State Street which served CCF for the next 23 years, housing 15-19 male students. In 1999 CCF purchased a large lot 1 block east of campus at the corner of 12th and Oak, and began construction of a new facility adjoining the existing 5 apartments. In 2000 the new Christian Campus House was completed at 112 E 12th Steet. The facility currently has offices and room for 31 residents in the main facility and 10 more in the pre-existing apartments.

Vision

Our vision for the “Forging A Legacy” campaign is to help us establish an even broader and deeper level of impact at Missouri S&T. This will be accomplished through an expansion of our current men's housing facility. We will be adding 9 suites allowing us to house a total of 64 male students. Our dining room will receive a makeover going from too small to functional with the ability for students to eat together, as well as hosting meals or events in the future. The construction will continue the current wrap around porch connecting to a spacious covered patio. This new patio on the back side of the building will create a perfect outdoor environment for bible study, worship, fellowship and relaxation.

Ministry Goals

It is our prayer that through a building expansion we will be better able and equipped to fulfill our mission statement and biblical mandate to reach others for His glory while doing Kingdom work.

Our addition will help us reach non-believing students by providing more space for outreach events. During O-Week, St Pats, spring break and other activities throughout the year, we will have expanded interior and exterior space for christian and non-christian students to develop relationships. The expansion will nurture fellowship among student believers. There will be more social areas for them to spend time with one another, experiencing and sharing life. There will also be room for additional male residents which will allow us to mentor and intentionally disciple more young men.

The new building will provide space for future growth of our staff. This will in turn help us lead the students to deepen their knowledge of and love for our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. Our prayer for this expanded facility is that our ministry would grow, extending our local and global impact, and through this, encouraging one another to serve and worship God using personal and spiritual gifts and talents for the kingdom and the spreading of the gospel.

Campaign Goals

  • Raise enough funds to pay for construction
  • Reach more godly young men
  • Reconnect with our alum and ministry supporters
  • Reduce debt to allow for additional staff
  • Ready the facility for use

Impact

The impact this addition will have on our ministry is immeasurable. Primarily, this facility will house more students. Due to lack of space, we have turned away many promising new applicants. We will have space for these young men and the facilities for their friends to fellowship with us. Currently there are five apartments adjoining the main house. These fatigued units are in continued need of maintenance which is becoming financially prohibitive. This addition will replace those five adjoining two-person apartments with nine four-occupant suites.

This expansion also brings a financial stability that provides the opportunity to better the minister to student ratio and provide adequate space to accommodate various activities throughout the year such as

  • Opening week (PUMBA!)
  • Night of praise
  • Friday Night Fellowship
  • Game nights
  • Officer and Board meetings
  • Alumni cookout (starting summer 2013)
  • Family cook-outs
  • Gel groups
  • Studying
  • and other recreational activities

Ultimately, this facility expansion will help us better meet the current and future needs that the ministries growth demands.
